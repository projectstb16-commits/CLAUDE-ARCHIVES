Internal Theft Protocol for Warehouse Operations — SOP Review
We have a 3-shift warehouse operation in Bekasi with approximately 140 staff. Over the past quarter we have noticed an 8% inventory discrepancy that cannot be attributed to system errors. Our current protocol only includes end-of-shift bag checks. What additional measures should we consider?
VH
The Vantage Hub Advisory
Security Consultant · Responded 6 Apr 2026

An 8% inventory discrepancy across a 3-shift operation with 140 staff is a significant signal. Based on your description, the most likely vectors are coordinated internal theft, collusion between shift supervisors and floor staff, or unauthorized after-hours access.

Immediate recommended actions: (1) Implement access log audits for the last 90 days — specifically cross-reference badge swipes during the 30 minutes before and after each shift change. (2) Review CCTV footage coverage at loading dock areas. Gaps here are the most common vulnerability in warehouse operations of your profile. (3) Introduce unannounced mid-shift inventory spot checks on high-value SKUs.

Structural SOP recommendations: Segregation of duties — the person authorizing outbound movements should not be the same person conducting the physical count. This single change reduces internal collusion risk significantly. Consider dual-sign-off for any movement above a defined threshold value.

I'm happy to review a draft of your updated SOP if you can share it in the next submission. We can also discuss CCTV placement strategy in your meeting this month.

✓ Answered Risk Management Submitted 28 Mar 2026
BCP Tabletop Exercise Format for a 200-Person Corporate Office
We need to conduct a mandatory BCP tabletop exercise for our Jakarta HQ before end of Q2. What format do you recommend and what scenarios should we include given our industry (FMCG distribution)?
VH
The Vantage Hub Advisory
Security Consultant · Responded 29 Mar 2026

For a 200-person FMCG distribution office in Jakarta, I recommend a half-day structured tabletop with 3 scenario rotations. The most relevant scenarios for your profile are: (1) warehouse fire or hazmat incident affecting distribution continuity, (2) active internal threat/fraud discovery during peak distribution period, and (3) IT system failure during month-end closing.

Key participants to include: Security Manager, HR Director, Finance Controller, Distribution Head, and at least one C-suite observer. The exercise should test your communication chain — specifically whether after-hours escalation to non-operational directors is clear and practiced.

I can provide a tabletop scenario template adapted for FMCG distribution — please request it as a follow-up and I'll prepare it for your next session.
